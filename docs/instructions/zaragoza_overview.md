# Zaragoza — City Overview
## Sep 1–2 (1 night stopover) · Euro Trip 2026

> One night between Barcelona and Madrid. Zaragoza is exactly halfway on the AVE high-speed rail. An underrated city with a jaw-dropping basilica, Moorish palace, Roman ruins, and almost no tourists.

### What to See (one afternoon + evening)

| Sight | Cost | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| **La Aljafería Palace** ⭐⭐ | €5 (free Fri afternoons) | 11th-century Moorish palace — Alhambra-level Islamic architecture almost unknown to tourists. Do not skip |
| **Basílica del Pilar** ⭐ | Free (towers ~€5) | Enormous Baroque basilica on the Ebro river. Twin towers can be climbed. Evening illumination spectacular |
| **La Seo Cathedral** | €5 | Gothic-Mudéjar cathedral, arguably more beautiful inside than El Pilar |
| **Museo del Foro de Caesaraugusta** | €4 | Roman forum underground beneath the city. Excellent audiovisual |
| **Casco Antiguo (Old Town)** | Free | Walk the medieval streets between La Seo and El Pilar cathedrals |

### Food (one dinner)

| Restaurant | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Casa Lac** ⭐ | Oldest restaurant in Spain (1825). Traditional Aragonese cuisine. Historic dining rooms |
| **El Chalet** | Modern creative Aragonese food |
| **Mercado Central** | 1903 iron market — browse before dinner |

> **Local specialty:** *Ternasco aragonés* — milk-fed Aragonese lamb, roasted. The dish of the region.
