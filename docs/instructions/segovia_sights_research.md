# Segovia — Sights & Things to Do
## Euro Trip 2026 — Day trip from Madrid (Sep 4)
### Avant from Madrid Chamartín → Segovia-Guiomar, 26 min · Return same evening

> Segovia is one of the most perfectly preserved medieval cities in Spain — a **Roman aqueduct that needs no cement**, a **fairy-tale Alcázar perched on a cliff**, the **last great Gothic cathedral** built anywhere in Spain, and an entire **medieval Jewish Quarter** preserved in stone. Twenty-six minutes from Madrid by high-speed train, **UNESCO World Heritage since 1985**, and despite the proximity to the capital it stays almost magically untouristed in the late afternoon once the bus groups leave.
>
> Mix of **essential sights** (Aqueduct, Alcázar, Cathedral) and **hidden corners** (Templar Vera Cruz, El Parral monastery, the Mirador below the Alcázar, San Justo's Romanesque frescoes) — try to do at least one from each category.

## 🏛️ Main Sights (essential)

| # | Name | Type | Cost | Time | Notes |
|---|------|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | **Roman Aqueduct** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Roman+Aqueduct+Segovia+Spain) ⭐⭐ | Roman monument (1st c. AD) | **Free** | 30–45 min | The most impressive Roman engineering left in Spain. **166 arches, 29 m high**, 17,000 granite blocks, **no mortar, no cement, no metal clamps**. Carried water 15 km from the Frío river. In active use until 1973. Best from Plaza del Azoguejo looking up; walk underneath; climb the stairs to the Mirador del Postigo above for the long view. **Morning light from the east is best.** |
| 2 | **Alcázar de Segovia** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Alc%C3%A1zar+de+Segovia+Segovia+Spain) ⭐⭐ | Medieval castle / royal palace | **€10/adult**, under 6 free; tower included | 1.5 hr | The castle Walt Disney visited before sketching Cinderella Castle. **Perched on a 80m granite wedge** at the confluence of the Eresma and Clamores rivers. Royal residence of Castile from the 12th century; site of **Isabella's coronation as Queen of Castile in 1474**; fire-destroyed in 1862 and faithfully restored. 10 reception rooms inside (Sala de las Piñas, Sala de los Reyes with all 52 Spanish monarch portraits, Throne Room). **The Torre de Juan II climb (152 narrow spiral-stair steps) gives the best view in central Spain** — old city + Guadarrama mountains + Aqueduct visible. Daily 10:00–20:00 (Apr–Oct). |
| 3 | **Segovia Cathedral** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Segovia+Cathedral+Segovia+Spain) ⭐ | Gothic cathedral (16th c.) | **€4/adult**, under 14 free | 40–50 min | **The last great Gothic cathedral built in Spain** — begun 1525 after the previous one was burned in the Comuneros' revolt. The chronological anachronism is the story: the rest of Europe was building Renaissance churches; Segovia rebuilt Gothic. Pointed arches, ribbed vaults, flying buttresses. Cloister stonework was salvaged from the burned predecessor (1410, dismantled and re-erected). Beautiful **walnut choir stalls** carved 1558. **Tower climb available — 90 m, separate ticket €3, panoramic view over the old city and out to Vera Cruz.** Daily 09:30–18:30, last entry 17:45. |
| 4 | **Jewish Quarter (La Judería)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Jud%C3%ADa+Vieja+Segovia+Spain) ⭐ | Medieval neighbourhood | Free | 30–45 min | Segovia had **one of Spain's largest medieval Jewish communities** before the 1492 expulsion (Edicto de Granada, signed by Isabella and Ferdinand right here in Segovia). The streets between the Cathedral and Alcázar preserve the medieval Jewish urban fabric — narrow lanes, low whitewashed houses, occasional original doorway lintels. Walk C/ de la Judería Vieja + C/ de la Judería Nueva. Look for the **Corpus Christi convent** at Plaza del Corpus (built directly on the foundations of the main synagogue — the medieval stonework is visible in the courtyard). |
| 5 | **Centro Didáctico de la Judería** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Centro+Did%C3%A1ctico+de+la+Juder%C3%ADa+Segovia+Spain) | Interpretive museum | **Free** | 20–30 min | Inside a restored 15th-century Jewish house at C/ de la Judería Vieja 12. Free overview of pre-1492 Sephardic life in Segovia — the original wooden ceiling, period domestic objects, an explanation of the cabbalistic decorative motifs that appear on Segovia building exteriors. Quick visit, good context before walking the quarter. |

## 🔍 Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path

> The corners of Segovia almost everyone misses — most day-trippers leave after the Aqueduct + Alcázar + cathedral. The hidden corners are where Segovia turns from a checklist into a place.

| Name | Type | Description | What to See | Cost | Hours |
|------|------|-------------|-------------|------|-------|
| **Iglesia de la Vera Cruz** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+la+Vera+Cruz+Segovia+Spain) ⭐⭐ | Templar church (12-sided) | **One of the only 12-sided churches in Spain.** Consecrated 1208, built by the Knights Templar (or possibly the Order of the Holy Sepulchre — records disagree). A **15-min walk down from the old city into the river valley** across the Eresma — almost no day-trippers make it here. Inside: a small central edicule held a fragment of the True Cross (now in a nearby village). Climb the tower for the **best long view back at the Alcázar across the valley.** | The 12-sided plan from outside; the central edicule; the tower climb (panoramic Alcázar) | €2.50/adult | Tue–Sun 10:30–13:30 + 16:00–19:00; closed Mon |
| **Mirador del Alcázar (valley floor)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mirador+del+Alc%C3%A1zar+from+below+Segovia+Spain) ⭐⭐ | Hidden viewpoint | The **classic Segovia postcard view** — Alcázar towering on the cliff above the Eresma, the old walls running back along the ridge. **Most day-trippers never find it.** Walk down from the Alcázar gates by the path to the valley (10–15 min descent), then continue across the river to the Mirador. | The Alcázar from below; sunset light on the cliff face | Free | Always open |
| **Monasterio de El Parral** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Monasterio+de+El+Parral+Segovia+Spain) ⭐ | Active monastery | A **15th-century Hieronymite monastery** in the Eresma valley, founded by **King Enrique IV of Castile** (Isabella's half-brother) in 1447. Still **actively inhabited by Hieronymite monks.** The church alone is open to visitors — Gothic interior with extraordinary 15th-century retablo. **Gregorian-chant Vespers daily at 19:00 in summer** — if your timing aligns, it's a singular experience. | The retablo; the church silence; **Gregorian chant Vespers** at 19:00 (silently sit at the back) | **Free** | Church Tue–Sat 10:00–13:30 + 16:30–19:00; Sun 09:15–13:30 |
| **Iglesia de San Martín** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+San+Mart%C3%ADn+Segovia+Spain) ⭐ | Romanesque church (11th c.) | One of Segovia's hidden Romanesque churches. **Exterior arcaded porch** — one of only a few surviving in central Spain. 13th-century tower with original Romanesque carved capitals. Quiet, dim, ancient. On a square just north of the Aqueduct on the way up into the old city. | The exterior porch; the carved tympanum over the south door; the dim interior | Free | Mon–Sat 10:30–13:30 (variable; may close in afternoon) |
| **Iglesia de San Justo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+San+Justo+Segovia+Spain) ⭐ | Romanesque church (12th c.) | A small **12th-century Romanesque church** just outside the old city walls (5 min from the Aqueduct), with **rare surviving 12th-century frescoes inside** — Christ Pantocrator in the apse, Last Supper, Resurrection scenes. **Almost completely unvisited by day-trippers.** | The apse frescoes (almost the only surviving Romanesque frescoes in Castile); the carved south doorway | €2/adult | Tue–Sat 10:30–13:30 + 16:30–19:00 (variable) |
| **Iglesia de San Esteban** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+San+Esteban+Segovia+Spain) | Romanesque church (13th c.) | Famous for its **soaring 53m bell tower** — said to be the most beautiful in all Spain's Romanesque architecture. Striking exterior, modest interior. | The exterior tower seen from below; the south porch carved capitals | Free | Mon–Sat ~10:30–13:30 |
| **Casa-Museo de Antonio Machado** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa-Museo+de+Antonio+Machado+Segovia+Spain) | Writer's house museum | The **preserved boarding-house room** where Spanish poet Antonio Machado (1875–1939) lived 1919–1932 while teaching French in Segovia. Three small rooms with his original desk, books, paintings. **Free, tiny, deeply moving** — Machado is one of Spain's most-loved poets, mostly because of his writing here. Guided visit (30 min, in Spanish). | His writing desk (he wrote *Los complementarios* here); the chronological room | Free | Tue–Sun 11:00–14:00 + 16:30–19:00 |
| **Cementerio Judío de El Pinarillo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cementerio+Jud%C3%ADo+El+Pinarillo+Segovia+Spain) | Medieval Jewish cemetery | A **pre-1492 Sephardic cemetery** — 100+ rock-cut graves on a wooded hillside above the Eresma. Used by Segovia's Jewish community for over 500 years. Most graves are simple rectangular pits cut directly into the granite slabs. Most visitors don't know it exists; a 10-min walk uphill from Vera Cruz. **One of the most affecting hidden corners in Segovia.** | The rock-cut graves; the silent forest setting; the view down to the river | Free | Always open (gate may close at dusk) |
| **Casa de los Picos** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+de+los+Picos+Segovia+Spain) | Renaissance palace | A 15th-century palace whose **entire facade is studded with carved granite pyramidal "spikes"** — 617 of them. Most spectacular Renaissance facade in Segovia. Houses an art school; **only the courtyard is accessible to visitors**, free. Walk through. | The studded facade; the inner courtyard | Free | Daily during business hours |
| **Casa de la Moneda (Royal Mint)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+de+la+Moneda+Segovia+Spain) | Industrial-history museum | **Spain's oldest royal mint** (1583, built by Felipe II) — on the banks of the Eresma below the Alcázar. Water-powered hammers and rolling mills (the technology was imported from the Tyrol). The first industrial-scale coin production in Spain. Honestly fascinating for older kids; quiet, almost no tourists. | The original water-driven hammer (still demonstrated occasionally); the mint coining-press | €3/adult | Tue–Sun 10:00–14:00 + 16:00–20:00 (summer) |
| **Puerta de San Andrés** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Puerta+de+San+Andr%C3%A9s+Segovia+Spain) | Medieval city gate | One of the **surviving medieval gates of Segovia's city walls** — at the south edge of the Jewish Quarter. The lower passageway houses a free interpretive centre about the walls + Jewish community. | The gate from outside the walls (best photo angle); free interpretive room | Free | Tue–Sun 10:30–14:00 + 16:00–18:00 |
| **Sinagoga Mayor (Convento del Corpus Christi)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Convento+del+Corpus+Christi+Segovia+Spain) | Converted synagogue / convent | The **main medieval synagogue of Segovia** — built ~13th century, converted to Catholic church 1410, now a cloistered convent of Clarisas. **The original synagogue stonework is still visible** in the courtyard and parts of the interior. Tours by prior booking only. | The original 13th-century synagogue stonework; the cloistered Catholic chapel layer on top | €2 (guided tour) | Limited tour slots — book at Segovia Tourist Office |
| **Mirador de la Pradera de San Marcos** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Pradera+de+San+Marcos+Segovia+Spain) | Picnic meadow + viewpoint | A grassy meadow at the foot of the Alcázar cliff, where you can lie on the grass and look up at the castle. Locals picnic here on Sundays. **The single best long-look-at-the-Alcázar from the ground.** | Lie back on the grass; long photos of the Alcázar | Free | Always |

A loop that hits 5 essentials + 4 hidden corners, with the cochinillo lunch in the middle:

```
1. Roman Aqueduct (free, 30–45 min)
        ↓
2. Iglesia de San Martín (free, 20 min)
        ↓
3. Segovia Cathedral (€4, 40 min)
        ↓
4. Jewish Quarter walk (free, 30 min)
        ↓
🐷 COCHINILLO LUNCH — Mesón de Cándido (booked, 13:30, 2 hr)
        ↓
5. Alcázar de Segovia (€10, 1.5 hr — includes tower climb)
        ↓
6. Mirador del Alcázar from below (free, 15 min walk down)
        ↓
7. Iglesia de la Vera Cruz (Templar, €2.50, 30 min)
        ↓

       → Taxi to Segovia-Guiomar, Avant back to Madrid
```

## ⚡ Quick-Pick Guide

| Goal | Answer |
|------|--------|
| **If you only do ONE thing** | Stand under the Roman Aqueduct — incomprehensible scale in person |
| **Best view (panoramic)** | Alcázar tower (Torre de Juan II) — 360° over old city + Guadarrama mountains |
| **Best classic photo of Segovia** | Mirador below the Alcázar — the postcard view |
| **Best hidden gem** | Iglesia de la Vera Cruz (Templar 12-sided church) or Monasterio de El Parral (15th-century Hieronymite monks) |
| **Most underrated** | Iglesia de San Justo (12th-century Romanesque frescoes inside) |
| **Most affecting** | Cementerio Judío de El Pinarillo (pre-1492 Sephardic graves cut into granite) |
| **Skip if short on time** | Casa de la Moneda; Casa-Museo de Antonio Machado |
| **Family favourite** | Alcázar tower climb + Plaza Mayor ponche + plate-smashing at lunch |
