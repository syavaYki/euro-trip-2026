# Zaragoza — Sights Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Sep 1–2 (1 night stopover)
### Arriving from Barcelona · Departing to Madrid the next morning

> One of Spain's most underrated cities. The capital of Aragón sits exactly halfway between Barcelona and Madrid on the AVE. Roman, Moorish, Mudéjar, and Gothic layers all stacked on top of each other — and almost no international tourists.

---

## 🏛️ Main Sights (one afternoon + evening)

| # | Name | Type | Cost | Time Needed | Notes |
|---|------|------|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | **Basílica de Nuestra Señora del Pilar** ⭐ | Enormous Baroque basilica on the Ebro river — the most visited pilgrimage site in Spain | Free (towers ~€5) | 45–60 min | Twin towers can be climbed for panoramic views. The tiled dome exterior is best seen from the Puente de Piedra bridge. Evening illumination across the river is extraordinary. Get there at dusk. |
| 2 | **La Aljafería Palace** ⭐ | 11th-century Moorish palace — extraordinary Islamic architecture, UNESCO | €5 (free Fri afternoons) | 1–1.5 hrs | One of the finest surviving Moorish palaces in Spain, comparable to the Alhambra but almost unknown internationally. Subsequent Christian layers (Gothic royal chapel, Catholic Monarchs' throne room) added on top. Truly spectacular — do not miss this. |
| 3 | **La Seo Cathedral (Catedral del Salvador)** | Gothic-Mudéjar cathedral, UNESCO — arguably more beautiful inside than El Pilar | €5 | 30–45 min | Built over a Roman forum and then a Moorish mosque — the archaeological layers are visible. The Mudéjar apse (outside, facing north) is one of the finest examples in Spain. Tapestry Museum inside is world-class. |
| 4 | **Museo del Foro de Caesaraugusta** | Excavated Roman forum under the city, with excellent audiovisual experience | €4; combo ticket with other Roman museums ~€7 | 30–45 min | The Roman city (Caesaraugusta) was one of the most important in Hispania. The forum ruins are preserved under an underground museum right in the city centre. |
| 5 | **Puente de Piedra** | 15th-century stone bridge over the Ebro — best view of El Pilar | Free | 15 min | Walk to the middle of the bridge at dusk for the classic basilica-reflected-in-river view. |
| 6 | **Casco Antiguo (Old Town)** | Medieval streets between the Seo and El Pilar cathedrals | Free | 1 hr | Very compact and walkable. The area around Calle Mayor and Plaza del Pilar is the heart of it. |
| 7 | **Mercado Central de Zaragoza** | 1903 Modernist iron market with stained-glass roof | Free entry | 20–30 min | Beautiful architecture. Browse before dinner — closed by evening, so visit on arrival. |

---

## 🗺️ One-Afternoon Route

**15:00 — Arrive Zaragoza Delicias station** → Tram or taxi to centre (~15 min, €10)

**15:30 — Mercado Central** → browse, orient yourself, snack

**16:00 — La Seo Cathedral** → Mudéjar exterior + interior + tapestry museum

**17:00 — Museo del Foro de Caesaraugusta** → Roman forum underground

**18:00 — Walk Plaza del Pilar** → see both cathedrals from the square

**19:00 — La Aljafería Palace** ⭐ → allow at least 1 hour (closes 20:00 in summer, check times)

**20:00 — Puente de Piedra** → dusk view of El Pilar across the river

**20:30 — El Pilar interior** → see it empty in the evening when pilgrims thin out

**21:30 — Dinner** in the Casco Antiguo

## ⚡ Quick-Pick

| Goal | Answer |
|------|--------|
| If you only do ONE thing | La Aljafería Palace — it's Alhambra-quality and you'll have it almost to yourself |
| Best photo | From Puente de Piedra at dusk with El Pilar reflected in the Ebro |
| Best free sight | Plaza del Pilar itself — the scale of El Pilar from the square is extraordinary |
| Best for history nerds | Museo del Foro de Caesaraugusta |
