# Zaragoza — Food Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Sep 1–2 (1 night stopover)
### One dinner + breakfast before Madrid train

> Zaragoza has a strong local food culture — *tapas de ternasco* (Aragonese milk lamb), *migas* (fried breadcrumbs with chorizo), *borrajas* (a local green), and some of the best cured meats in Spain. The pintxos scene on Calle El Temple is modest but decent.

---

## 🍽️ Dinner Options (one evening)

| Name | Description | What to Order | Price Range | Address | Notes |
|------|-------------|--------------|-------------|---------|-------|
| **Casa Lac** ⭐ | Established 1825 — the oldest continuously operating restaurant in Spain. Classic Aragonese cuisine in a beautiful historic room. | *Ternasco al horno* (roast milk lamb); *migas aragonesas*; seasonal game | €30–45/person | C/ de los Mártires 12 | Book ahead — tables fill even on weeknights. Historic without being stuffy. |
| **El Chalet** | Modern creative Aragonese cuisine. One of Zaragoza's best restaurants for contemporary cooking with local ingredients. | Tasting menu or à la carte seasonal dishes | €40–60/person | C/ de Santa Teresa de Jesús 25 | Book ahead. |
| **La Rinconada de Lorenzo** | Classic Aragonese home cooking. The *ternasco* and *truchas del Pirineo* (Pyrenean trout) are outstanding. | *Ternasco asado*; *trucha a la Navarra*; house croquetas | €25–35/person | C/ de la Salle 3 | Walk-in often fine but book to be safe. |
| **El Temple pintxos street** | Calle El Temple is Zaragoza's answer to Carrer de Blai — a street of pintxos bars. Less famous but very local. | Mix of pintxos from 3–4 bars | €15–20/person | C/ del Temple area | Go 20:30–22:00. More low-key than Barcelona's version. |
| **Mercado de las Artes** | Modern food market / hall in a 1920s space. Multiple stands — good option for a lighter meal. | Browse stalls | €15–25/person | C/ de San Ignacio de Loyola | More relaxed, good for families |

| Name | What | Notes |

## 🥩 Must-Try Aragonese Dishes

| Dish | What It Is | Where |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Ternasco aragonés** | Milk lamb (under 30 days old) — the PDO product of Aragón. Roasted simply with garlic and olive oil. Astonishingly tender. | Casa Lac, La Rinconada |
| **Migas aragonesas** | Fried stale breadcrumbs with chorizo, bacon, garlic, and egg. Peasant food elevated to an art form. | Traditional restaurants |
| **Trenza de Almudévar** | Braided almond pastry — Zaragoza's signature pastry. | Fantoba pastry shop |
| **Jamón de Teruel** | Teruel (1 hr away) produces Spain's second most famous cured ham (after Jabugo). | Any good bar or restaurant |
| **Bolaños de La Codoñera** | Local small peppers, pickled — perfect with vermouth at a bar | Local bodegas |
