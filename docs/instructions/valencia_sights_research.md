## 🏛️ Main Sights (one afternoon/evening + morning)

| # | Name | Type | Cost | Time Needed | Notes |
|---|------|------|------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | **Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias** ⭐ | Santiago Calatrava's spectacular science city complex — futuristic bone-white architecture on a dried riverbed | €8–36 depending on attractions; L'Oceanogràfic alone ~€32/adult | 2–4 hrs | The exterior is completely free to walk around and photograph — extraordinary architecture. L'Oceanogràfic (Europe's largest aquarium) takes half a day. Hemisfèric IMAX adds more. Worth seeing even just the exterior on evening arrival. |
| 2 | **Valencia Cathedral + El Miguelete Tower** ⭐ | Gothic cathedral housing the Holy Grail (the actual chalice used at the Last Supper per Church tradition) | Cathedral free; tower €2.50; Holy Grail chapel free | 45–60 min | This is THE Valencia sight for a family that loves churches and history. The Holy Grail is displayed in a side chapel — small alabaster cup, 1st-century Roman, remarkable regardless of what you believe. Climb El Miguelete bell tower for the best old-town view. |
| 3 | **Mercado Central** ⭐ | 1928 Modernista iron-and-tile market under a spectacular dome — the most beautiful market in Spain | Free entry | 30–45 min | Go in the morning before 11:00. Stalls of paella ingredients, fresh seafood, cured meats, saffron. One of the great market buildings in Europe. Don't miss the central dome. |
| 4 | **Barrio del Carmen** | Valencia's medieval quarter — bohemian, painterly, full of street art, independent bars, and hidden courtyards | Free | 1 hr | The neighbourhood to wander on the evening of arrival. Street art covers most surfaces. The Plaça del Tossal and Plaça del Negrito are where locals congregate at night. |
| 5 | **L'Oceanogràfic** | Europe's largest aquarium, inside the City of Arts and Sciences | ~€32/adult; ~€23/child | 2.5–3 hrs | If the family did the Lisbon Oceanário, this is bigger and different — more emphasis on open-water species. Worth it if arriving early enough; skip if arriving late Aug 27. |
| 6 | **Jardins del Turia** | 9km of gardens built in the old Turia riverbed after it was diverted in 1957 — the world's longest urban park | Free | 30 min–2 hrs | Extraordinary public space. Cycling, walking, football pitches, playgrounds, and Gulliver Park (giant Gulliver sculpture with slides built into it — free, kids love it). The river gardens connect the Old Town to the City of Arts. |
| 7 | **La Malvarrosa Beach** | Valencia's city beach — wide, sandy, the birthplace of paella | Free | Half day if time allows | On morning of Aug 28, a 7am swim before the train is absolutely doable — metro to Neptú stop, 20 min from centre. The beach where paella was invented (by agricultural workers who cooked by the lagoons here). |
| 8 | **Llotja de la Seda (Silk Exchange)** ⭐ | Gothic UNESCO World Heritage Hall — one of the finest Gothic secular buildings in Europe (1482–1548) | ~€3/adult; under 12 free | 30–45 min | The Sala de Contratación (trading hall) with its helical columns is extraordinary. Very uncrowded even in August. Often closed in the heat of midday — go early morning. |
| 9 | **Real Basílica de la Virgen de los Desamparados** | Baroque basilica next to the Cathedral on the Plaza de la Virgen | Free | 20 min | Beautiful frescoed dome. Very atmospheric and free. The Plaza de la Virgen itself (with the Roman-era fountain) is one of Valencia's best squares. |

## 🗺️ One Evening + One Morning Route

**Evening (Aug 27 — arrive afternoon):**
- Drop bags → walk to **Barrio del Carmen** → wander the street art streets
- **Valencia Cathedral** + Holy Grail (open until 18:30 or 19:30)  
- **Plaza de la Virgen** → see the Basilica + Roman fountain
- Sunset walk to **Ciudad de las Artes exterior** (~20 min from centre) → extraordinary light on the white buildings
- Dinner in El Carmen or near the market (see food file)

**Morning (Aug 28 — before 11:25 train):**
- **07:00** Optional: quick swim at La Malvarrosa beach (metro 20 min each way, €1.50)
- **09:00** **Mercado Central** — open from 07:30, best before 11:00
- **09:45** **Llotja de la Seda** (10 min walk from market)
- **10:30** Walk or taxi to **Estació del Nord** (15 min walk)
- **11:25** Train departs → Barcelona 2:50 PM
