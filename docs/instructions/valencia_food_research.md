# Valencia — Food Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Aug 27–28 (1 night stopover)
### One dinner + breakfast before Barcelona train (11:25 AM)

> Valencia is the birthplace of paella. This is non-negotiable: you eat paella in Valencia. Not a tourist paella — a real one, cooked in a traditional iron pan over a wood fire, with rabbit and chicken (the original), or seafood (the coastal version). The city also has the best *horchata* in the world (made from tiger nuts, not dairy) and *agua de Valencia* (a local cocktail the whole city is proud of).

---

## 🥘 The Paella Imperative

> **Rule #1:** Paella is a lunch dish in Valencia. Never dinner. Any restaurant serving paella at 9pm for tourists is not a real paella house. If you're arriving late Aug 27, target a restaurant that cooks to order at midday the next day — but actually the train is at 11:25, so this is tricky. **Option: paella dinner at a tourist-friendly traditional restaurant** (see below), or **skip paella and do horchata + local snacks** and save the paella for Barcelona where it's also very good.

## 🥤 Must-Try Valencian Things

| Item | What It Is | Where |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Horchata de chufa** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Horchata+de+chufa+Valencia+Spain) ⭐ | Cold drink made from tiger nuts (*chufa*) — nutty, sweet, creamy, completely non-dairy. Valencia's signature. Nowhere else in Spain does it like this. | Horchatería Santa Catalina (Plaza Santa Catalina 6, since 1936) or Horchatería Daniel |
| **Fartons** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Fartons+Valencia+Spain) | Soft elongated pastry for dipping in horchata. Always eaten together. | Same horchaterías |
| **Agua de Valencia** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Agua+de+Valencia+Valencia+Spain) | House cocktail of Valencia: cava + orange juice + vodka or gin. Every bar serves it. Kids get the OJ version. | Any bar in the Old Town |
| **Paella valenciana** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Paella+valenciana+Valencia+Spain) | The original: chicken + rabbit + butter beans + tomato, cooked in a flat pan over wood fire. NOT seafood. | See restaurants above |
| **Arròs al forn** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Arr%C3%B2s+al+forn+Valencia+Spain) | Oven-baked rice with pork ribs, chickpeas, blood sausage, garlic — Valencian winter dish that appears on menus year-round | Traditional restaurants |
| ***All i pebre** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=All+i+pebre+Valencia+Spain)* | Eel and potato stew with garlic, paprika, and chilli — traditional fisherman's dish from the Albufera lagoon | Traditional restaurants |
| **Bunyols** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Bunyols+Valencia+Spain) | Fried dough fritters — Valencia's version of churros | Cafés and pastry shops |

## 🍽️ Dinner Recommendation (Evening Aug 27)

For a one-night stop where paella at lunch isn't feasible, the best move is dinner in the **Ruzafa** neighbourhood (Valencia's creative/foodie district, 15-min walk from centre):

| Name | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Canalla Bistro** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Canalla+Bistro+Valencia+Spain) | Ricard Camarena's casual restaurant — creative tapas, global influences, fun atmosphere. Very popular. | Book ahead; C/ de Maestro Serrano 7 |
| **El Poblet** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Poblet+Valencia+Spain) | Ricard Camarena's fine dining. Michelin-starred. A proper splurge. | Book weeks ahead |
| **La Fusta** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=La+Fusta+Valencia+Spain) | Traditional Valencian food in the Ruzafa neighbourhood. Local crowd. | Walk-in usually fine |
| **Mercado de Colón** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mercado+de+Col%C3%B3n+Valencia+Spain) | Beautiful 1916 modernist building turned food hall. Good drinks and light dinner option. | Passeig de Russafa |
