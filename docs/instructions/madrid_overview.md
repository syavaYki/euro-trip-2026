## Getting Around

- **Metro:** 12 lines, extremely extensive. **10-trip Metrobús card** (~€12.20, shared across 4 people for 2.5 trips each), or single tickets €1.50–2 depending on zone. Atocha + Sol + Legazpi are the relevant stations. Most sights are 1–2 metro stops apart or walking distance.
- **Walking:** Centre is genuinely walkable. From Legazpi: 15 min to La Latina, 20 min to Palacio Real or Prado, 25 min to Plaza Mayor.
- **Bolt / Uber / Cabify:** All operate citywide; €5–12 across the centre. Useful with kids tired at end of day.
- **Airport:** Metro L8 Aeropuerto T4 → Nuevos Ministerios (35 min, €5–6 including airport supplement) — but **on Sep 5 morning a taxi flat-rate €30 from Arganzuela** is the right move.
- **Weather Sep 1–5:** 26–32°C daytime, 16–19°C at night. Dry. Sunset ~20:15. Pleasant evenings — pack one layer for after dark.

## Day 2 — Wednesday Sep 2 · Royal Madrid + Templo de Debod Sunset

> Palacio Real → Almudena Cathedral → Plaza Mayor → Mercado de San Miguel lunch → afternoon hidden church (San Antonio de los Alemanes) → Templo de Debod for sunset.

**08:00 — Breakfast**

Walk 10 min to **Café Comercial** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Caf%C3%A9+Comercial+Madrid+Spain) (Glorieta de Bilbao) — Madrid's oldest café (1887). Or 5-min walk to a local bar for *café + tostada con tomate* (€3). For a special start: **Chocolatería San Ginés churros con chocolate** (15 min walk / metro 4 stops to Sol).

**09:30 — Walk / metro to Plaza de Oriente**

Metro L3/L6 Legazpi → L3 Sol → walk 8 min via C/ del Arenal. Or taxi (~€7, 12 min). **Aim to be at Palacio Real ticket gate by 09:50** — opens 10:00.

**10:00 — Palacio Real** ⭐⭐ (pre-booked)

Royal Palace tour: Throne Room (Tiepolo ceilings), Hall of Halberdiers, Banquet Hall (145-piece dinner service), **Royal Armoury** (kids' jaws drop — child-sized parade armour, jousting suits, 16th-century cannons). Allow **2 hours**. €14/adult; under 5 free.

> ⚠️ **Wednesdays + Saturdays 11:00–14:00:** Changing of the Guard outside, every 30 min. Linger at the Plaza de la Armería to catch one.

**12:15 — Catedral de la Almudena** ⭐ (across the plaza)

Free entry. **Don't skip the crypt** (separate entrance on C/ Mayor side — free, often empty). 400 Neo-Romanesque columns; Madrid's patroness Virgen de la Almudena. ~45 min for cathedral + crypt.

**13:15 — Plaza de Oriente + walk to Plaza Mayor**

Walk through Plaza de Oriente (statues of all the Visigoth kings of Spain), down C/ Mayor — 12 min downhill stroll.

**13:30 — Lunch: Mercado de San Miguel** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mercado+de+San+Miguel+Madrid+Spain) ⭐

Graze across stalls: oysters (€2.50/each at Daniel Sorlut), jamón ibérico cone, croquetas, vermut, sushi. Standing at marble counters. ~€20/person. Allow 1h.

**Alternative (if you want sit-down):** Walk 3 min to **Taberna La Bola** for *cocido madrileño* — but you must have **booked ahead** (call +34 91 547 69 30). Cocido is best as the main event of the day, so book this for Day 3 lunch instead.

**14:45 — Plaza Mayor** ⭐

3-min walk. Stand at the centre, look up at Casa de la Panadería frescoes. 9 archways out. 15–20 min. **Mid-afternoon: less crowded than 11:00 or 19:00.**

**15:15 — Heladería Palazzo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Helader%C3%ADas+Palazzo+Madrid+Spain) (C/ del Arenal 1)

3-min walk from Plaza Mayor. Gelato break. €3–5/cone. Eat walking.

**15:30 — Walk to San Antonio de los Alemanes** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+San+Antonio+de+los+Alemanes+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐ (hidden gem)

15-min walk north via Gran Vía. **The most surprising interior in Madrid** — plain brick exterior, then ceiling-to-floor baroque frescoes by Luca Giordano (1689). Sit in a pew. Look up. Free. Open Mon–Thu 10:30–13:30 + 17:30–19:30; **closed Fri afternoon and Sat afternoon** — Wed at 16:30 is perfect.

**16:30 — Coffee + walk through Malasaña**

Walk through Plaza del 2 de Mayo, C/ de la Palma. Optional **HanSo Café** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=HanSo+Caf%C3%A9+Madrid+Spain) for a flat white (~€4).

**17:30 — Mercado de San Antón** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mercado+de+San+Ant%C3%B3n+Madrid+Spain) (Chueca)

10-min walk. 3-floor food market. Walk through the produce floor, gelato at **Mistura** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mistura+helader%C3%ADa+Madrid+Spain) on the ground floor (basil sorbet, pistachio + sea salt). 30 min.

**18:30 — Walk to Templo de Debod** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Templo+de+Debod+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐

20–25 min walk west through Gran Vía → Plaza de España → Parque del Oeste. Or metro: Tribunal L1 → Plaza de España L3 (5 min).

**19:30 — Templo de Debod (sunset)**

Sep 2 sunset is **~20:18.** Arrive 19:30 to claim a spot on the west-facing terrace. Genuine 2nd-century BC Egyptian temple. Reflecting pools mirror the orange sky. Casa de Campo + Guadarrama mountains behind. **The view of the trip.** Free.

**20:30 — Dinner: Sobrino de Botín** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Sobrino+de+Bot%C3%ADn+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐ (must book ahead)

Walk 15 min south or grab a taxi (~€7) to C/ de los Cuchilleros 17 — **world's oldest restaurant (1725)**. The 300-year wood-fired oven roasts **cochinillo asado** (carved tableside) and **cordero asado**. 4-floor medieval cellar. €40–55/person.

> **Alternative if Botín is fully booked:** **Posada de la Villa** (Cava Baja — wood-fired lamb), or **Casa Lucio** (Cava Baja — huevos rotos con jamón — book 2 weeks ahead).

**22:30 — Walk back via Plaza Mayor at night** (lit, almost empty), then **gelato chaser at Palazzo Arenal** if still energy. Taxi home from Sol (~€8).

## Day 3 — Thursday Sep 3 · Art + Old City + Retiro

> Prado morning (pre-booked) → Madrid's signature dishes for lunch → Reina Sofía & Guernica → Retiro rowboats at sunset → tapas dinner in La Latina (Cava Baja bar-hop).

**08:00 — Breakfast at San Ginés** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Chocolater%C3%ADa+San+Gin%C3%A9s+Madrid+Spain) ⭐

Metro Legazpi L3 → Sol L1 (15 min). **Chocolate con porras** at the white-marble counter. Open 24h — at 08:00 it's empty and magic. €5/person.

**09:30 — Walk to the Prado** (15 min via Carrera de San Jerónimo)

Stop at **Casa Mira** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+Mira+Madrid+Spain) (turrón shop since 1855) on the way for a peek at the preserved 19th-century shopfront.

**10:00 — Museo del Prado** ⭐⭐ (pre-booked timed entry)

Go **straight to Room 12 — *Las Meninas***. Then Goya's Black Paintings (Rooms 67–68), then *Saturn Devouring His Son*, then Bosch's *Garden of Earthly Delights* (Room 56A), then El Greco (Room 10B). 2.5–3 hours total. Audio guide (€5) is excellent for kids.

**13:00 — Walk through CaixaForum + vertical garden**

Exit south from Prado, walk 5 min to **CaixaForum Madrid** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=CaixaForum+Madrid+Spain) — Herzog & de Meuron's floating-brick building + Patrick Blanc's 24m vertical garden across the street. Photos. 20 min.

**13:30 — Lunch: Taberna La Bola** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Taberna+La+Bola+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ (must book ahead — limited daily portions)

Metro Banco de España L2 → Ópera (8 min) or taxi (~€8). The temple of *cocido madrileño*: 3-stage service of chickpea-stew-with-everything. Cooked in individual clay pots over wood fire. €25–35/person. Cash for lunch. Book +34 91 547 69 30 a week ahead.

> **Alternative if no booking:** **Casa Labra** near Sol (battered cod tapas, €3–4/each — the Madrid pilgrimage), or **Casa Dani tortilla** at Mercado de la Paz (Salamanca, metro Serrano L4, +5 min) — call ahead to confirm open.

**15:30 — Walk to Real Jardín Botánico** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Real+Jard%C3%ADn+Bot%C3%A1nico+Madrid+Spain)

Metro Ópera L5 → Banco de España L2 (5 min) → walk 10 min. €6/adult (kids free; Tue free). Cool, shaded, 5,000 plant species. Bonsai pavilion. 1h.

**17:00 — Museo Reina Sofía** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Museo+Reina+Sof%C3%ADa+Madrid+Spain) ⭐

5-min walk from the Botanical Garden. **Guernica is the reason.** Stand in front of it 10 minutes. Then Dalí's *The Great Masturbator* and *Girl at the Window*. €12/adult (kids free). 1.5h.

**18:30 — Walk to Parque del Retiro** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Parque+del+Retiro+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐

15-min walk via Atocha. Or metro L2 Banco de España → Retiro.

**19:00 — Retiro rowboats on the Estanque** ⭐⭐

**The single best family activity in Madrid.** €6/boat for 30 min (up to 4 people). Two girls each on an oar. Take the obligatory family photo at the central monument. Then walk through the gardens to the **Palacio de Cristal** (glass-and-iron pavilion, free contemporary art inside).

**20:00 — Walk back through Retiro to dinner**

Exit at Puerta de Alcalá → C/ de Alcalá → Plaza de Cibeles. Optional **Mirador Cibeles rooftop** (€3, panoramic sunset over Gran Vía — but you've earned the dinner break).

**20:45 — Dinner: Cava Baja bar-hop in La Latina**

Metro Sol L1 → La Latina L5 (5 min). The most fun "moving dinner" in Madrid — pick 3 of the following, 1–2 dishes + drink at each:

| Stop | What | Price |
|------|------|-------|
| **Casa Lucio** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+Lucio+Madrid+Spain) | **Huevos rotos con jamón** — the dish. Book ahead | €25/person |
| **Posada de la Villa** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Posada+de+la+Villa+Madrid+Spain) | Wood-fired lamb if not done at Botín | €40/person |
| **Bodegas Ricla** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Bodegas+Ricla+Madrid+Spain) (C/ Cuchilleros 6) | Wine from the barrel + embutidos | €10/person |
| **Casa Toni** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+Toni+Madrid+Spain) (C/ de la Cruz 14) | *Oreja a la plancha* for the brave | €10/person |

**23:00 — Walk back through Plaza Mayor (lit, quiet) → taxi to apt** (€8). Gelato chaser at any open Palazzo or Mistura on the way.
