# Madrid — Sights & Experiences Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Sep 1–5 (4 nights · 3 full days + Segovia day trip)
### Family of 4: Seva, wife, 2 daughters

> ⚠️ **Critical closure checks before the trip:**
> - **Ermita de San Antonio de la Florida (Goya frescoes + tomb)** — closed for conservation since 17 March 2025, no reopening date confirmed. **Verify at [esmadrid.com](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/ermita-de-san-antonio-de-la-florida) before going.** If reopened by Sep 2026 this becomes one of Madrid's essential sights.

## ⛪ Churches & Religious Sites (family priority)

> The family loves churches, cathedrals, chapels — Madrid has extraordinary ones, mostly **free** and **completely uncrowded**. Best visited in **morning light** (10:00–11:00) for the dome glow, or right before evening Mass (~19:00) when locals come and the candles are lit.

## ⚰️ Historic Cemeteries (family priority)

> The family loves historic cemeteries. Madrid's two great Victorian-era necropolises are extraordinary — both **free**, both **uncrowded**, both walkable from the centre or one metro stop away.

| Name | Description | Don't Miss | Cost | Hours | Address |
|------|-------------|-----------|------|-------|---------|
| **Cementerio de la Almudena** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cementerio+de+la+Almudena+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | **Spain's largest cemetery** and **one of the largest in Western Europe** — 120 hectares, ~5 million graves (more than the current population of Madrid). Opened 1884 after the 1834 cholera epidemic forced burials outside the city walls. Long avenues lined with **family pantheons in every architectural style** — Neoclassical, Egyptian Revival, Modernista, Gothic Revival, Art Nouveau. Resting place of writer Pío Baroja, composer Joaquín Rodrigo, bullfighter Manolete, and the heart of Saint Isidore (the body is in San Isidro church). Vast and silent. | The Modernist **panteones** along Avenida Principal; the **Egyptian Revival pyramid** of the Marqueses de Hinojares; the Russian Orthodox section near the back wall. | **Cementerio de la Sacramental de San Isidro** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cementerio+Sacramental+de+San+Isidro+Madrid+Spain) | **Madrid's most prestigious 19th-century cemetery** (founded 1811, parish of San Isidro). Smaller than Almudena but **architecturally denser** — full of Romantic and Modernista mausoleums. Goya was first buried here (1828, before exhumation to San Antonio de la Florida); the **dukes of Alba, the poet José Zorrilla, Mariano José de Larra, painter Federico de Madrazo** all rest here. Often called Madrid's Père-Lachaise. | **Panteón de los Hombres Ilustres** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Pante%C3%B3n+de+los+Hombres+Ilustres+Madrid+Spain) | A small **neo-Byzantine pantheon** (1899) attached to the basilica of Atocha — Spain's failed answer to Westminster Abbey.
