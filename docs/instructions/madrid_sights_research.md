| Name | Category | Description | Must-Do / Insider Tip | Cost (€/adult) | Hours | Address | Website |
|------|----------|-------------|----------------------|----------------|-------|---------|---------|
| **Museo del Prado** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Museo+del+Prado+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ 📖 | Museum / Fine Art | Spain's national art museum — one of the world's greatest, housed in a neoclassical palace. Over 8,000 works spanning the 12th–19th centuries. Under-18s always free. | Free Mon–Sat 18:00–20:00 and Sunday 17:00–19:00. Book timed entry in advance for August — queues without tickets can exceed 1 hour. | **Museo Reina Sofía** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Museo+Reina+Sof%C3%ADa+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | Museum / Contemporary Art | Home to Picasso's *Guernica* — the defining anti-war artwork of the 20th century and Madrid's most important single object. Closed Tuesdays. | Stand in front of *Guernica* for 10 minutes — read the story first so the imagery lands fully. | **Museo Thyssen-Bornemisza** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Museo+Thyssen-Bornemisza+Madrid+Spain) | Museum / Fine Art | Bridges the gap between the Prado (pre-19th century) and Reina Sofía (20th century). Houses one of the world's greatest private art collections — from early Flemish masters through Impressionists to Pop Art. Less crowded than the Prado. | The Thyssen completes the "Golden Triangle of Art" with the Prado and Reina Sofía — all three are within a 10-minute walk of each other. | €13 (under 18 free; free Mon) | Mon 12:00–16:00; Tue–Sun 10:00–19:00 | Pg. del Prado 8, 28014 Madrid | [museothyssen.org](https://www.museothyssen.org/en) |

Gardens (Jardines de Sabatini and Campo del Moro) are free and spectacular. | Arrive at 10:00 to beat queues. Combine with a walk through the free Jardines de Sabatini and a stop at the mirador for views over the Casa de Campo. Under 5 free. | €14 (under 5 free; free Wed/Thu for EU citizens) | Apr–Sep: Mon–Sat 10:00–20:00; Sun 10:00–17:00 | C/ de Bailén s/n, 28071 Madrid | [patrimonionacional.es](https://www.patrimonionacional.es/en/real-sitio/palacio-real-de-madrid) |
| **Parque del Retiro** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Parque+del+Retiro+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ 👧 | Park | Madrid's great central park — 118 hectares of formal gardens, tree-lined promenades, the famous Estanque lake with rowboats, glass Crystal Palace, sculptures, and free outdoor concerts on weekends. The most beloved public space in Madrid. | Rent rowboats on the Estanque (€5–8, one of the essential Madrid experiences). Find the free Crystal Palace (Palacio de Cristal) hosting rotating contemporary art exhibitions. | **Plaza Mayor** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Plaza+Mayor+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | Square | Magnificent 17th-century arcaded square at the heart of old Madrid. Site of bullfights, markets, executions, and canonizations through history. | Go early morning (8am) before tourist masses — completely different atmosphere. The arcade cafés are overpriced; buy coffee from a bar on a side street instead. | Free | Always open | Plaza Mayor, 28012 Madrid | — |
| **Puerta del Sol** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Puerta+del+Sol+Madrid+Spain) | Square / Landmark | Madrid's geographic and symbolic centre — the 0km marker for all Spanish roads. | **El Rastro (flea market)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Rastro+%28flea+market%29+Madrid+Spain) 👧 | Flea Market | Madrid's legendary Sunday morning street market — one of the largest in Europe. | **Templo de Debod** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Templo+de+Debod+Madrid+Spain) | Egyptian Temple / Viewpoint | A genuine 2nd-century BC Egyptian temple donated to Spain by Egypt in 1968 — relocated stone by stone. The temple is free from the outside; interior visits by timed entry. | Free (exterior); timed entry for interior | Tue–Sun 10:00–14:00 and 18:00–20:00 | Pg. del Pintor Rosales, 28008 Madrid | — |
| **Mercado de San Miguel** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mercado+de+San+Miguel+Madrid+Spain) | Food Market | Stunning early 20th-century iron and glass market hall near Plaza Mayor, converted into an upscale *mercado gourmet*. More beautiful than La Boqueria, less chaotic, slightly more expensive. | Arrive when it opens at 10am — it gets very crowded by noon. Good for grazing lunch or afternoon tapas. | Free entry | Mon–Thu 10:00–24:00; Fri–Sat 10:00–02:00; Sun 10:00–24:00 | Plaza de San Miguel 1, 28005 Madrid | [mercadodesanmiguel.es](https://www.mercadodesanmiguel.es/en/) |

## 🔍 Hidden Gems & Off-the-Beaten-Path

| Name | Type | Description | What to Do / See |
|------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **El Capricho de La Alameda** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Capricho+de+La+Alameda+Madrid+Spain) | Secret Park | One of the most extraordinary gardens in Spain — 18th-century Duchess of Osuna's whimsical retreat, with a labyrinth, bridges, neoclassical pavilions, and a romantic lake. Open only on weekends. Almost entirely tourist-free. | Visit Saturday or Sunday morning; walk the full circuit of pavilions and find the Casita del Labrador. |
| **Matadero Madrid** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Matadero+Madrid+Madrid+Spain) | Arts Centre | Massive former municipal slaughterhouse (1920s) converted into a sprawling cultural campus — theatre, contemporary art exhibitions, film screenings, a design library, and a vast outdoor terrace. Genuinely local scene. | Come on a summer Friday evening: outdoor drinks, gallery openings, and usually something live happening. |
| **Lavapiés** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lavapi%C3%A9s+Madrid+Spain) | Neighbourhood | Madrid's most multicultural and artistically vibrant neighbourhood. Cramped 19th-century streets full of flamenco bars, international restaurants (Korean, Moroccan, Ethiopian), independent theatres, and street art. The most genuinely diverse corner of the city. | Wander Calle del Ave María; find the Filmoteca Española cinema; eat at one of the Indian or Senegalese restaurants on Calle Embajadores. |
| **El Rastro side streets** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Rastro+side+streets+Madrid+Spain) | Market Area | The main El Rastro flea market is overwhelming — but the side streets (particularly Calle del Carnero and around) host specialist dealers: vintage vinyl records, old maps, military memorabilia, and antique ceramics. | Break away from the main Ribera de Curtidores strip and explore the specialist stalls. |
| **San Isidro catacombs** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=San+Isidro+catacombs+Madrid+Spain) | Underground Site | Under the Capilla de San Isidro, Madrid's patron saint's chapel, lie underground chambers rarely visited by tourists. The chapel is ornate, small, and genuinely serene compared to the adjacent San Andrés church. | Free; best visited on a Tuesday morning when the area is quiet. |

## 🏘️ Neighbourhoods

| Name | Vibe | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| **Salamanca** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Salamanca+Madrid+Spain) | Upscale, tree-lined boulevards, designer boutiques, Mercado de la Paz. The most elegant neighbourhood; excellent restaurants. Best family base. | Shopping; Prado access; good restaurants; quiet streets |
| **La Latina** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=La+Latina+Madrid+Spain) | Medieval streets, tapas bars, El Rastro on Sundays. The most atmospheric old-Madrid neighbourhood. Dense tapas culture. | Sunday El Rastro + tapas; evening bar-hopping on Cava Baja |
| **Malasaña** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Malasa%C3%B1a+Madrid+Spain) | 1980s counterculture district turned hipster hub. Independent cafés, vintage shops, record stores, cocktail bars. Best specialty coffee neighbourhood in Madrid. | Breakfast; vintage shopping; evening cocktails |
| **Lavapiés** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lavapi%C3%A9s+Madrid+Spain) | Multicultural, artistic, edgy. Diverse food scene, theatre, street art. The real Madrid bohemia. | International food; street art; flamenco bars |
| **Retiro / Atocha** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Retiro+%2F+Atocha+Madrid+Spain) | Elegant residential area bordering the park. Museum Golden Triangle. Wide, manageable streets. Good for museum days. | Art museums; park; Atocha station (glass garden inside) |
