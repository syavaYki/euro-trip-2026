# Segovia — City Overview
## Sep 4 (day trip from Madrid) · Euro Trip 2026

> One of the most perfectly preserved medieval cities in Spain — a **1st-century Roman aqueduct** built without a drop of mortar, a **cliff-top Alcázar** that inspired Disney's Cinderella castle, a Gothic cathedral that's the last great medieval cathedral built in Spain, and the dish the whole city exists for: ***cochinillo asado***. **26 minutes from Madrid Chamartín by Avant train.** Mix of obvious essentials and hidden corners — Templar church, Sephardic cemetery, monastery on the river.

### 10:00 — Roman Aqueduct ⭐⭐

You exit the bus at **Plaza del Azoguejo** and look up. **166 arches, 29 metres at its highest point**, made of granite blocks fitted together without mortar, without cement, without a single drop of any binder — pure precision stonecutting from the 1st century AD. It carried water 15 km from the Frío river and **was in active use until 1973** — still working after 1,900 years.

Walk the **full length** from Plaza del Azoguejo back along the rising arches toward the source, then return underneath. The scale only registers when you walk both views. Standing directly beneath an arch and looking up is the moment.

### 11:00 — Walk up into the Old City

Climb the medieval staircase (the **Postigo del Consuelo**) on the right side of the Aqueduct — opens immediately into the old town's quieter back streets. Or walk up gradually via Calle de Cervantes. 10 min uphill.

---

### 11:15 — Iglesia de San Martín (hidden church gem)

A short detour at the top — **Iglesia de San Martín** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Iglesia+de+San+Mart%C3%ADn+Segovia+Spain), an 11th-century Romanesque church with an exterior arcaded porch (one of the only ones surviving in central Spain) and a 13th-century tower. Free interior usually 10:30–13:30. Quiet, dim, ancient. 20 min.

---

### 11:45 — Segovia Cathedral ⭐

The **last great Gothic cathedral built in Spain** — begun **1525** after the previous one was burned in the Comuneros' revolt against Charles V. Pointed arches and ribbed vaults inside, **flying buttresses outside** — already an anachronism in 1525 when the rest of Europe was building Renaissance. Beautiful carved walnut choir stalls. Cloister reused from the burned predecessor cathedral.

| Don't miss | Cloister; carved choir stalls; the *Tríptico de las Ánimas* by Ambrosius Benson |

### 12:35 — Jewish Quarter (La Judería) wander ⭐

Segovia had **one of Spain's largest medieval Jewish communities** before the 1492 expulsion. The streets between the Cathedral and the Alcázar preserve the medieval Jewish layout. Walk **C/ de la Judería Vieja** and **C/ de la Judería Nueva** — narrow cobbled lanes, low whitewashed houses, the occasional original doorway.

Look for the **Corpus Christi convent** — built **on the foundations of the main synagogue**; the lower stonework is still visible in the courtyard. **Centro Didáctico de la Judería** (free entry) at C/ de la Judería Vieja 12 has a 30-min overview if interested.

Quick photo stop at the **Puerta de San Andrés** — surviving medieval city gate at the south edge of the Judería.

### 13:30 — Cochinillo Lunch ⭐⭐ (book weeks ahead!)

***Cochinillo asado*** — whole **suckling pig (max 21 days old, weighing 4–5 kg)**, slow-roasted in a wood-fired clay oven at 250°C for 90–120 minutes until the skin shatters like glass and the meat falls off the bone. By tradition, **the waiter carves it using the edge of a ceramic plate** — to prove how tender it is — then **smashes the plate** on the floor.

This is the dish in Segovia. The only one that matters. You came for this.

Also order alongside: **judiones de La Granja** (giant white beans from the nearby town, slow-cooked with chorizo — Segovia's other signature), **sopa castellana** (garlic-paprika-bread-egg soup), and **ponche segoviano** (the city's signature pastry — layered sponge + marzipan + cream) for dessert. Plus an Aragonese or Castilian red. Allow **2 hours.**

### 15:30 — Alcázar de Segovia ⭐⭐

Walt Disney visited Segovia before designing Cinderella Castle — and the resemblance is so obvious that the Alcázar's silhouette literally is one of the inspirations. The **wedge-shaped fortress sits on a dramatic cliff at the exact confluence of the Eresma and Clamores rivers** — sheer drops on three sides. Built as a royal palace in the 12th century, rebuilt and expanded through the 16th, lived in by Spanish kings, then served as artillery academy from 1762 until a fire in 1862 destroyed it (faithfully reconstructed). Where **Isabella was crowned Queen of Castile in 1474**.

The interior runs through 10 reception rooms — Sala de las Piñas (gilded ceiling), Sala de los Reyes (gallery of 52 Spanish monarch portraits), the Throne Room. But the real reason to visit:

**Torre de Juan II** — **152 narrow spiral-staircase steps** to a 360° panoramic terrace: the entire old city walled up on the ridge below, the **Guadarrama mountains** behind, the Roman Aqueduct visible in the distance. **Madrid's best view-from-a-tower.**

### 17:00 — Mirador below the Alcázar ⭐ (hidden corner)

Walk down the steep path from the Alcázar gates into the river valley below — a 10–15 min descent through pine woods. From the valley floor you get the **classic Segovia postcard**: the cliff-face Alcázar towering above the Eresma river, the old city walls running back along the ridge. **The most photographed view in Segovia, and most day-trippers never find it.**

The path passes the **Casa de la Moneda** (Spain's oldest royal mint, 1583 — €3 to enter; skip if pressed) and crosses the river on a small stone bridge before climbing the opposite slope.

---

### 17:30 — Iglesia de la Vera Cruz ⭐ (Templar gem, hidden corner)

A 15-minute uphill walk from the Mirador (or **5-min taxi** if legs are done): the **12-sided Romanesque Templar church**, consecrated 1208, built by the Knights Templar (or possibly the Order of the Holy Sepulchre — the records disagree). It's **one of the only 12-sided churches in Spain.** Inside: a tiny central edicule with the relic of the True Cross brought back from Jerusalem. Climb the tower for the **best long-distance view back across the valley at the Alcázar** — almost no one else here.

### 18:15 — Monasterio de El Parral (optional hidden gem) [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Monasterio+de+El+Parral+Segovia+Spain)

If energy holds, a 5-min walk from Vera Cruz: an active **15th-century Hieronymite monastery** in the river valley, founded by **Enrique IV of Castile** (Isabella's half-brother). The **church alone is open to visitors** — a quiet Gothic interior with extraordinary 15th-century retablo. Gregorian-chant Vespers daily at 19:00 in summer — if you happen to be there, it's a singular experience. Free entry to the church.

> Skip if cochinillo lunch ran long — Vera Cruz + cathedral are the priorities.

---

### 19:00 — Plaza Mayor + Ponche Segoviano

Taxi back up (~€7) or walk uphill 15 min. **Segovia's main plaza** is quieter and more proportioned than most — arcaded, the cathedral on one side, the 16th-century **Casa Consistorial** (city hall) and the Romanesque **Iglesia de San Miguel** (where Isabella was proclaimed Queen of Castile in 1474) on the others.

Order **ponche segoviano** at any café — **layered sponge + marzipan + cream + glazed top**, made only in Segovia. Plus a *café con leche*.

> **Family note (per memory):** No bars/clubs — café-and-pastry on the Plaza Mayor is exactly the family pace.

---

~€8, 15 min. **Be on the platform 10 min before departure** — Renfe closes access 2 minutes before. The 20:00 Avant or the 20:35 are both fine.
