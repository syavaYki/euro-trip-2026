# Madrid Food & Drink Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Sep 1–5 (4 nights)
### Family of 4: Seva, wife, 2 daughters

> 🗓️ **Early September timing notes:** Madrid is hot but dry (24–30°C); evenings cool to ~18°C and the city eats outdoors. Many locals are still on August holidays through the first week of September — some classic restaurants close all of August and reopen Sep 1–8. **Phone ahead the week of the trip** to confirm reopening for any place you really want to hit (Casa Dani, Botín, La Casa del Abuelo, Sobrino de Botín).
>
> **Family rules in play:** no bar crawls, no clubs — sub in gelato stops, evening miradouro walks, family-friendly restaurants. Madrid has an extraordinary gelato culture; plan 2 stops/day in the heat.

## 🥐 Breakfast & Café Spots

> Madrid's breakfast ritual is *café con leche + tostada con tomate* (toasted bread, fresh-rubbed tomato, olive oil, salt) standing at a counter — total ~€3. Or churros + chocolate at any hour. The specialty-coffee wave reached Malasaña around 2018 and is now excellent.

## 🍦 Gelato & Ice Cream

> Madrid's gelato scene is anchored by two long-running family chains (Palazzo since the 1970s, Mistura since 2014) and a wave of newer artisan spots. **Plan 2 stops per day in the 30°C heat.**

| Name | Why Go | Location(s) | Price | Family Rating | Tip |
|------|--------|-------------|-------|---------------|-----|
| **Mistura** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Mistura+helader%C3%ADa+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐ | Portuguese for "mixture" — Madrid's most-talked-about artisan gelato. Five locations citywide. You build your scoop by picking a flavour + add-ins; staff folds it on the cold marble in front of you. Fruit sorbets are made from whole fresh fruit daily. | C/ del Barquillo 36 (Chueca); C/ Argensola 16 (Chueca); Mercado de San Antón (Chueca); C/ Goya 35 (Salamanca); C/ Caracas 25 (Chamberí) | €3.50–6 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Pistachio + sea salt is the signature; basil sorbet is the surprise winner. Take it to Plaza de Chueca and eat in the square. |

## 🥩 Tapas & Madrid Classics

> The Madrid tapas tradition: order a *caña* (small draft beer) or *vermut* and a free tapa appears with it — for €2.50–4 you're fed. Order more if you want more. **Cava Baja** (La Latina) is the densest tapas street; **Calle Ponzano** (Chamberí) is where locals actually eat now.
