## 🥩 Tapas & Madrid Classics

| Name | Description | Must-Try / Featured Item | Price Range | Address | Website |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Casa Dani** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+Dani+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | Legendary tortilla bar inside the Mercado de la Paz in Salamanca. Queues form before it opens. The *tortilla española* is a world benchmark — creamy, slightly undercooked in the centre (*jugosa*), perfectly seasoned. Cash only. Go early. | *Tortilla española jugosa* — one of the best in Spain | €3–5 (tortilla slice) | Mercado de la Paz, C/ de Ayala 28, 28001 Madrid (Salamanca) | Walk-in, cash only |
| **La Casa del Abuelo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=La+Casa+del+Abuelo+Madrid+Spain) | Famous for *gambas al ajillo* (prawns in garlic olive oil) since 1906. Four locations; order the house sweet red wine (*La Casa del Abuelo Vino*) alongside. Dive straight in at opening — queues grow fast. | *Gambas al ajillo*; *gambas a la plancha* (grilled prawns); house sweet red wine | €15–25/person | Multiple locations (original: C/ de la Victoria 12, near Sol) | [lacasadelabuelo.es](https://www.lacasadelabuelo.es/) |
| **Cava Baja** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cava+Baja+Madrid+Spain) (street) ⭐ | La Latina's main tapas street — the densest and best concentration of traditional tapas bars in Madrid. Start at one end and work down. Best 17:00–19:00 (before the crowds) or 21:00+ (local timing). | Bar hop: 1–2 tapas + drink per bar across 4–5 bars on the street | €15–25/person | Calle de la Cava Baja, 28005 Madrid (La Latina) | — |
| **Carambola** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Carambola+Madrid+Spain) | Family-friendly tapas restaurant in central Madrid with a good kids' menu, relaxed atmosphere, and creative Catalan-inflected tapas. A reliable option when travelling with children. | *Patatas bravas*; *pimientos de padrón* (fried peppers); *pan con tomate* | €18–25/person | C/ de Atocha 67, 28012 Madrid (Centro) | — |
| **Bodegas Ricla** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Bodegas+Ricla+Madrid+Spain) | Ancient wine bar (est. 1860) near La Latina — one of Madrid's oldest. Dark, dusty, barrel-aged wines served straight from the barrel. *Embutidos* (cured meats) and cheese. Step back in time. | House wine from the barrel (€1.50–2/glass); jamón ibérico | €10–15/person | C/ de Cuchilleros 6, 28005 Madrid (La Latina) | — |
| **El Brillante** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=El+Brillante+Madrid+Spain) | Institution near Atocha station for *bocadillo de calamares* — Madrid's signature street food: a crusty roll filled with fried calamari rings. The quintessential cheap Madrid snack. | *Bocadillo de calamares*; cold *Mahou* beer | €3–5/sandwich | C/ del Doctor Drumen 7, 28012 Madrid (Atocha) | — |

## 💃 Flamenco Dining

| Name | Description | Must-Try / Featured Item | Price | Address | Notes |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|---------|-------|
| **Flamenco de Leones** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Flamenco+de+Leones+Madrid+Spain) | Flamenco dinner show in central Madrid — tapas + dinner + live performance. The earlier sitting (20:00–21:00) works best with children — the show is intense and passionate, not too late. Book ahead in August. | Dinner + flamenco show (combined ticket) | €40–60/person | C/ de los Leones, Centro | Book ahead; earlier sittings for families |

More upscale than tourist-oriented shows. | Flamenco show + dinner or drinks | €45–75/person | C/ de Echegaray 15, 28014 Madrid | [cardamomo.es](https://www.cardamomo.es/en/) |

## 🍽️ Dinner Restaurants
