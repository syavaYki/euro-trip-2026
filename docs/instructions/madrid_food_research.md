# Madrid Food & Drink Research
## Euro Trip 2026 — Sep 1–5 (4 nights)
### Family of 4: Seva, wife, 2 daughters

> 🗓️ **Early September timing notes:** Madrid is hot but dry (24–30°C); evenings cool to ~18°C and the city eats outdoors. Many locals are still on August holidays through the first week of September — some classic restaurants close all of August and reopen Sep 1–8. **Phone ahead the week of the trip** to confirm reopening for any place you really want to hit (Casa Dani, Botín, La Casa del Abuelo, Sobrino de Botín).
>
> **Family rules in play:** no bar crawls, no clubs — sub in gelato stops, evening miradouro walks, family-friendly restaurants. Madrid has an extraordinary gelato culture; plan 2 stops/day in the heat.

## 🥩 Tapas & Madrid Classics

> The Madrid tapas tradition: order a *caña* (small draft beer) or *vermut* and a free tapa appears with it — for €2.50–4 you're fed. Order more if you want more. **Cava Baja** (La Latina) is the densest tapas street; **Calle Ponzano** (Chamberí) is where locals actually eat now.

### 🍷 Calle Ponzano — Where *Madrileños* Actually Eat Now

> A 600-metre street in Chamberí lined with 30+ tapas bars and *cocinas*. Until 2015 it was a working-class strip; today it's where locals queue and tourists rarely venture. Plan **bar-hop dinner**: 4–5 bars, 1–2 dishes each.

| Stop | What | Price |
|------|------|-------|
| **Sala de Despiece** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Sala+de+Despiece+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | High-concept "butcher's table" — staff in butcher's coats serve raw and just-cooked beef dishes. Standing counter, queue, theatrical. *Carpaccio de gamba*. | €15–25/person |
| **Tripea** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tripea+Madrid+Spain) | Modern bites from a market stall counter; chef Roberto Martínez. | €10–20/person |
| **Arallo Taberna** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Arallo+Taberna+Madrid+Spain) | Galician seafood-tapas hybrid; pulpo a la plancha. | €15–25/person |

> **Madrid's three signature dishes:**
> - **Cocido madrileño** — chickpea stew with chorizo, morcilla, pork belly, hen, beef, vegetables — served in 3 stages (broth → chickpeas → meats). La Bola, El Sur, or Ciriaco (Wed).
> - **Huevos rotos / estrellados** — fried potatoes + runny eggs + jamón, smashed together. Casa Lucio is the temple.
> - **Bocadillo de calamares** — fried-squid sandwich. El Brillante or any bar around Plaza Mayor.
> - **Cochinillo asado** — saved for Segovia (Day 4) — but Sobrino de Botín does it world-class right here too.

## 💃 Flamenco Dining

> Madrid is the place to see flamenco performed authentically. Earlier sittings (20:00 vs 22:30) work for kids. **Dinner is included** in the tablao price; you can also book "drinks only" tickets for ~€20 less.

| Name | Description | Must-Try / Featured Item | Price | Address | Notes |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|---------|-------|
| **Corral de la Morería** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Corral+de+la+Morer%C3%ADa+Madrid+Spain) ⭐⭐ | The most celebrated tablao in the world (est. 1956). Where every flamenco great — Antonio Gades, Camarón, Lola Flores — has performed. Tiny stage, 60 seats, an audience three metres from the dancers. Two parallel rooms: the *Tablao* (show + drinks) and the **Michelin-starred Gastronómico** (tasting menu + show). | Show + drinks; or pre-show dinner | Show €52/person; show + tasting menu €130+ | C/ de la Morería 17, 28005 Madrid (La Latina) | [corraldelamoreria.com](https://corraldelamoreria.com/) |
| **Cardamomo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Cardamomo+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | Critics' pick for **authentic flamenco** (the dancers are working artists, not a tourist troupe). Three shows nightly (18:00, 20:00, 22:30) — the 18:00 or 20:00 works best for the family. Smaller and more intimate than the grand tablaos. | Show + tapas; or show + dinner | €40–75/person | C/ de Echegaray 15, 28014 Madrid (Las Letras) | [cardamomo.es](https://www.cardamomo.es/en/) |
| **Tablao Flamenco 1911** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Tablao+Flamenco+1911+Madrid+Spain) | Formerly Villa Rosa — the oldest active tablao in the world (since 1911). Closed and rebranded 2020. Beautiful tiled interior on Plaza Santa Ana. Less star-power than Corral, but the building and lineage are extraordinary. | Show + tapas | €45–55/person | Plaza Santa Ana 15, 28012 Madrid | [tablaoflamenco1911.com](https://www.tablaoflamenco1911.com/) |
| **Las Tablas** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Las+Tablas+Madrid+Spain) | Less famous, less expensive, very good. Located near Plaza de España. Two daily shows, decent dinner. The "budget right answer" for families. | Show + drink; show + dinner | €30–55/person | Plaza de España 9, 28008 Madrid | [lastablasmadrid.com](https://www.lastablasmadrid.com/) |

> **Family pick:** **Cardamomo 20:00 show with tapas pairing** — done by 22:00, kids manage, the flamenco is the real thing. Book 1–2 weeks ahead.

## 🍫 Hidden-Gem Sweet Stops

| Name | What | Address |
|------|------|---------|
| **Antigua Pastelería del Pozo** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Antigua+Pasteler%C3%ADa+del+Pozo+Madrid+Spain) | **Madrid's oldest bakery — since 1830.** Hand-painted ceiling, mahogany counters, tiled floor, original wood-fired oven. *Empanadas de bonito*, *roscones*, *bartolillos*. Untouched in 195 years. Closed weekends. | C/ del Pozo 8, 28012 Madrid (Sol) |
| **La Violeta** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=La+Violeta+Madrid+Spain) | Since 1915. The shop that invented **violet candies** — sugared crystallised violet flowers, in tiny tin boxes. The favourite sweet of King Alfonso XIII. Buy a tin as a present. | Plaza de Canalejas 6, 28014 Madrid |
| **Casa Mira** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Casa+Mira+Madrid+Spain) | Since 1855 on Carrera de San Jerónimo. The Madrid temple of *turrón* (Christmas almond nougat) — but they sell it year-round. Beautiful preserved shopfront. | Carrera de San Jerónimo 30, 28014 Madrid |
| **Convento del Corpus Christi (Las Carboneras)** [📍](https://maps.google.com/?q=Convento+del+Corpus+Christi+Madrid+Spain) ⭐ | Cloistered nuns sell home-made *pastas* and cookies through a **turnstile in the wall** — you can't see them, they can't see you, you put cash in the wheel and the wheel turns back with biscuits. Buzz the bell on Plaza Conde de Miranda. Surreal, hidden, intensely Madrid. | Plaza del Conde de Miranda 3, 28005 Madrid |
