## 🏖️ Beaches

> Barcelona has 4.5km of city beaches — the most accessible urban beaches of any major European city. Best before 10:00 or after 18:00 in August.

| Beach | Transport | Why | Family Notes |
|-------|-----------|-----|--------------|
| **Nova Icària** ⭐ | Metro L4 to Ciutadella–Vila Olímpica | Calm water, lifeguards, showers, playgrounds, volleyball nets, chiringuitos (beach bars). Best all-round family beach. | Sheltered cove, shallow entry — ideal for kids. Well-organised, clean. |
| **Bogatell** | Metro L4 to Poblenou | Wide, less crowded than Barceloneta, good facilities. Popular with locals rather than tourists. | More space, good for families who want more room. |
| **Barceloneta** | Metro L4 to Barceloneta | Most central; most crowded. Iconic but very packed in August. Better for a quick dip than a full beach day. | Crowded; can feel overwhelming with kids. Better to go to Nova Icària or Bogatell. |
| **Castelldefels** | R2 train from Sants (~25 min, €4) | Wide Atlantic-style sand, much quieter than city beaches, great for a half-day escape. | Calm, wide, and significantly less crowded than Barcelona beaches in August. |

## 🏘️ Neighbourhoods

| Name | Vibe | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| **Eixample** | Modernista grid, wide boulevards, most major Gaudí buildings. Best base for families — Metro everywhere, wide pavements, shops and restaurants everywhere. | Gaudí sightseeing; shopping; late dinner |
| **El Born / El Barri Gòtic** | Medieval streets, boutique shops, Picasso Museum, nightlife. Most walkable historic area. | Walking, history, tapas crawls |
| **Gràcia** | Village-feel neighbourhood above Eixample. Squares with outdoor seating, local bars, park Güell nearby. Calm, local, excellent food. | Local evening dinner; relaxed exploration |
| **Poble-sec** | Hillside neighbourhood at the foot of Montjuïc. Dense tapas bar strip on Carrer de Blai (pintxos). Budget-friendly, increasingly cool. | Pintxos crawl; Montjuïc base |
| **Barceloneta** | Former fishing district stretching out into the sea. Beach, chiringuitos, paella restaurants, seafood. Lively and touristy. | Seafood lunch; beach afternoon |

## 🚌 Day Trips from Barcelona

| Destination | Transport | Duration | Description | Family Notes |
|-------------|-----------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Montserrat** | FGC train from Plaça Espanya (~1h, €25 return with rack railway) | Half–full day | Dramatic serrated mountain with a Benedictine monastery at 725m.
