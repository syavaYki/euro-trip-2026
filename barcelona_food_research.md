## 🥘 Tapas, Pintxos & Local Classics

| Name | Description | Must-Try / Featured Item | Price Range | Address | Website |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Carrer de Blai (pintxos street)** ⭐ | Poble-sec street lined with pintxos bars — Basque-style open-faced snacks on bread, €2–3 each. The most fun cheap eating in Barcelona. Kids pick their own from displays at the bar. Go 18:00–20:00 before the late rush. | Mix of pintxos from 3–4 bars along the street; order cañas (small beers) to accompany | €2–3/pintxo | Carrer de Blai, 08004 Barcelona (Poble-sec) | — |
| **Ciudad Condal** | Lively tapas restaurant on Rambla de Catalunya, always buzzing. One of Barcelona's most reliable for high-volume, high-quality Catalan and Spanish tapas. No reservations — join the queue. | Patatas bravas; *croquetas* (croquettes); *gambas al ajillo* | €18–25/person | Rambla de Catalunya 18, 08007 Barcelona | — |
| **Quimet & Quimet** | A Barcelona institution — tiny, standing-room only, serving extraordinary *montaditos* (creative tapas on bread) loaded with tinned fish, conservas, and unexpected toppings. Only open lunchtimes (closed evenings + Sundays). | Sardines + yogurt + honey montadito; tuna + caviar; canned conservas tapas | €15–20/person | C/ del Poeta Cabanyes 25, 08004 Barcelona (Poble-sec) | — |
| **Bodega Quimet** | Old-school neighbourhood wine bar in Gràcia. Dark, wood-panelled, cured meats hanging from the ceiling. Serves *vermouth* (traditional Sunday aperitivo), excellent cheap wines, *embotits* (cured meats), and *encurtidos* (pickles). | Vermouth + *jamón* + local cheese; *embotit* platter | €12–18/person | C/ de Vic 23, 08006 Barcelona (Gràcia) | — |
| **Bodega Neus** | Gràcia neighbourhood bodega known for the *bomba* — a Barcelona original: a spiced potato-and-meat ball, deep-fried, served with two sauces (one spicy, one alioli). Also famous for *bikini* (toasted ham and cheese sandwich). | *Bomba*; *bikini* sandwich | €12–18/person | C/ de Bonavista 10, 08012 Barcelona (Gràcia) | — |

## 🍷 Vermouth Culture (Aperitivo Hour)

> Barcelona's *vermut* (vermouth) culture is a midday ritual — Saturday and Sunday 12:00–15:00. Bars serve house vermouth on ice with an olive and a small tapa. Essential experience.

| Name | Description | What to Order | Address |
|------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| **Bar Calders** | Best neighbourhood vermut bar in Sant Antoni. Terrace tables, local crowd, excellent house vermouth. | Vermut amb sifó (vermouth + soda); *boquerones* (anchovies) | C/ del Parlament 25, Sant Antoni |
| **El Xampanyet** | Historic cava bar in El Born — one of the oldest in Barcelona. Famous for house *xampanyet* (sparkling cava) and anchovies. | Xampanyet sparkling; anchovies + olives | C/ de Montcada 22, El Born |
| **Bar Marsella** | Absinthe bar in El Raval, operating since 1820 — unchanged decor, dusty antique bottles, old Hemingway legends. The oldest bar in Barcelona. | House absinthe (served the traditional way with a sugar cube) | C/ de Sant Pau 65, El Raval |
